MY TOP FAVORITE FILIPINO FOODS

About

In this personal blog webpage activity, I want to talk about my favorite Filipino foods. I decided to choose this content because I love foods and so here, I want to share what I think about my favorite Fillipino foods.


Filipino food is a mix of Asian and Western influences transformed through local cooking techniques. Boasting proud indigenous flavors, rich traditions and eating practices unique to the country, Filipino cuisine is distinct, delicious and unlike anything you'll have tasted elsewhere.

Chicken Adobo

"Hello Adobo!"

    Of course! Your Filipino favorites will not be completed without the famous Adobo. Everyone in the country has their own version of it but my favorite is the Chicken Adobo. I love it best when my mom uses Mang Tomas sauce as replacement to soy sauce. It adds a little sweetness to it, and I love sweet foods.

    Just a little information for those who didn't know, Chicken Adobo is a Filipino dish made by braising chicken legs (thighs and/or drumsticks) in a sauce made up of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and black pepper. It's tangy, salty, garlicy, slightly sweet, and spicy. The chicken is slowly simmered in the sauce making it flavorful and incredibly tender.

    Main Ingredients:

    • Chicken
    • Soy sauce
    • Vinegar
    • Black pepper


Sinigang na Bangus

"Asim Kilig!"

    Sinigang na Bangus is a type of sour clear broth fish soup. You can really enjoy the fresh taste of the fish complemented by the warm sour broth. If you are trying to learn how to cook, this dish is a perfect choice to start with.

    Most say that this is the most authentic representation of Filipino cuisine. What I love in this dish is its sour soup, which everyone can enjoy especially during rainy seasons.

    Main Ingredients:

    • Bangus
    • Siling haba
    • Vinegar(or any other sour-tasting products)
    • Tomato


Lumpiang Shanghai

"Hmmm, perfection!"

    Lumpiang Shanghai is undeniably the all time favorite Filipino food. It is always present in almost all Filipino celebration and fiestas, and everyone, including me, always looks for it. In fact, it is slowly becoming a meme in social media in a way that if it's not present in a celebration, don't bother to invite me. I myself, also consider Lumpiang Shanghai as my favorite Filipino food.

    So for those who didn't know, here's a quick facts again. Lumpiang Shanghai is a Filipino-style spring roll made of ground pork, chicken or beef filling wrapped in thin crepes called lumpia wrappers. The meat-stuffed lumpia is rolled into a thin cylindrical shape, cut into bite-size lengths and deep-fried to golden perfection.

    Main Ingredients:

    • Lumpia wrappers
    • Ground pork
    • Minced onions and carrots
    • Seasonings such as salt and ground bell pepper


Chicken Tinola

"Wow sabaw!"

    Aside from Sinigang, Chicken Tinola is also my all time favorite Filipino soups. Its leafy components is what I like the most about it. Tinola is also a flexible dish since you can choose any vegetables to combo with it and will always works. My favorite one is the Malungay leaves.

    Classic Tinolang Manok is a healthy Filipino chicken soup dish with vegetables. This dish has a clear broth and includes moringa (malunggay), chayote, green papaya, hot pepper leaves, and ginger as part of the ingredients. Tinolang Manok is also known as chicken tinola.

    Main Ingredients:

    • Chicken parts(mainly legs)
    • Green papaya or chayote
    • Hot pepper leaves
    • Ginger


Pork Sisig

"Sisig for the win!"

    Oh yes, sisig! What I love about this dish is its crispyness and flexibility. Everyone can enjoy this delicious dish no matter wha tradition you have. From pork, chicken, or to fish, you can still enjoy the flavor of the Filipino sisig. My favorite and also the mojority, is the Pork Sisig. I think how this dish was made was so creative. It has the balance of crispyness, sweetness, and spicyness.

    A quick information Pork Sisig is a Filipino food made from minced pork. This Sisig Recipe makes use of minced pigs ears and meat (pork belly). Sisig originated in Pampanga, a place a few kilometers North of Manila. Aside from using ears and belly, the pig's face (also called mascara) is used to make sisig.

    Main Ingredients:

    • Pig parts
    • Onions
    • Chili
    • Fried egg
